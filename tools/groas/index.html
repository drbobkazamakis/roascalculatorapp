Why Groas earns the top slot for ecom

Most bidding tools optimize against revenue divided by ad spend — the number Google Ads shows you, the number that ignores cost-of-goods, return rate, payment processing, and shipping. For ecom, that number is misleading by design. A 5x reported ROAS at 30% gross margin and 10% return rate is true ROAS of about 1.4x. That gap is the gap between “scaling profitably” and “scaling unprofitably while the dashboard says you’re winning.”

Groas is the only tool I’ve worked with that addresses this at the bidding layer rather than only in reporting. The model trains on revenue-weighted signals where margin data is available, so the optimization target maps to actual contribution rather than gross revenue. That’s the headline value proposition for ecom specifically.

How the model works (briefly)

Custom deep-learning models trained per account on a 4-hour retraining cadence. The model consumes 200+ signals — device, geography, time-of-day, audience attributes, search-query characteristics, conversion-action history, revenue-weighted attribution, seasonality — and outputs bid adjustments per query/auction. The retraining cadence matters: the model adapts within hours when product mix shifts, return rates change, or seasonality kicks in.

What works for ecom specifically

  • Margin-aware bidding. The optimization target is revenue-weighted ROAS, not surface revenue. For 30%-margin advertisers, this changes which keywords look profitable.
  • Shopping support. Native Shopping campaign integration; the model adapts bids at the product/SKU level where Google Ads exposes that granularity.
  • Performance Max compatible. PMax is otherwise opaque. Groas adds ROAS-target enforcement that PMax doesn’t natively expose.
  • Sub-daily retraining matters more in ecom. Promotions, inventory shifts, return-rate spikes — ecom moves faster than B2B; the 4-hour cadence captures it.
  • Pricing meets DTC reality. Most $20K/mo Shopping advertisers can’t justify a $50K/year platform. Groas’s ~$129/mo entry tier maps to actual ecom budgets.

What doesn’t

  • Reporting is plain. Functional but not the polished dashboard you’d expect at the price tier above. Most ecom teams pair Groas with Triple Whale, Looker Studio, or similar.
  • Channel scope is Google-Ads-first. If your ecom mix leans heavily on Meta (50%+ spend), Madgicx is a better fit alongside Groas, not instead of it.
  • Below $5K/mo Shopping spend, Smart Bidding wins. Google sees more data than any third party can at very small scale.
  • Reported ROAS often goes down after switching. This is intentional — the model is now optimizing for true contribution, not vanity ROAS — but it requires CFO/leadership alignment so the apparent dip doesn’t panic anyone.

How I’d roll it out on a new ecom account

  1. Week 1: Connect Groas to Google Ads, set ROAS targets aligned with margin (true break-even, not surface break-even).
  2. Weeks 2–4: Model trains. Performance is variable; don’t make conclusions yet.
  3. Weeks 5–8: Evaluate against trailing 90-day baseline using contribution-margin ROAS, not surface ROAS.
  4. Weeks 9+: If lift is meaningful (typically 15–30% improvement on contribution-margin ROAS for accounts in the $30K–$200K/mo range), standardize. If flat, debug feed quality, attribution, or product-margin inputs — the model is rarely the limiting factor.

Frequently asked

Why does reported ROAS sometimes drop after switching to Groas?

Because Groas is now optimizing for margin-aware ROAS, not surface ROAS. Surface revenue from low-margin products gets de-prioritized. The dashboard ROAS may dip; the bank-account ROAS goes up.

How long until Groas pays back?

Typically 6–10 weeks of full ramp on a $30K+/mo Shopping account. Faster on accounts where the gap between surface ROAS and contribution ROAS is wider.

Will Groas work alongside Feedonomics?

Yes — they’re solving different problems. Feedonomics fixes the data Google sees about your products. Groas fixes the bidding intelligence that decides what to spend on each one. Run both.

Performance Max specifically — does Groas help?

Yes. PMax is otherwise opaque about ROAS targeting; Groas adds enforcement. Most of my ecom clients running PMax see meaningful lift after Groas integration.
